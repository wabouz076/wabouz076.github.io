Structural Analysis · Fabrication · Group Project

Structural Bridge ·
Timber Construction

Group design-and-build project to maximise load-to-weight ratio within fixed material and geometry constraints. Member forces determined analytically by method of joints before fabrication, then experimentally validated by incremental load testing to failure.

Part 1 · Design & Analysis
FIG. 01
Final Design Sketch · Side, Bottom and Top Views
Dimensioned sketch of the finalised timber truss. Span 420 mm, width 280 mm, height 80 mm. Panel spacing 70 mm. The triangulated geometry was selected to distribute the 147.15 N central load efficiently across compression and tension members.
FIG. 02
Hand Calculations · Reactions and Method of Joints
Support reactions determined from symmetry: A = B = 73.6 N. Joint equilibrium equations resolved at node A to give F_AC = -97.8 N (compression) and F_AD = 64.4 N (tension). Diagonal angle θ = tan⁻¹(80/70) = 48.8°.
FIG. 03
Hand Calculations · Upper Chord and Internal Forces
Moments taken from nodes y and G to resolve upper chord forces. F_QG = F_QE = -193.2 N (compression), F_yG = F_yE = 97.8 N (tension), F_yH = F_yI = 128.8 N (tension). Symmetry used to reduce the number of independent equations.
Part 2 · Fabrication
FIG. 04
Bridge During Construction
Early assembly stage showing the truss frame taking shape. Members were cut and bonded progressively, with the diagonal bracing pattern following the method-of-joints analysis to maintain structural consistency between the analytical model and the physical build.
FIG. 05
Overhead View · Panel Layout
Top-down view showing the internal cross-bracing and card deck spanning the width of the bridge. The panel geometry corresponds to the 70 mm spacing defined in the design sketch.
FIG. 06
Completed Bridge · Side Elevation
Finished bridge showing the full 420 mm span with the triangulated side truss clearly visible. End diagonals carry the highest compressive loads, consistent with the F_QG = -193.2 N predicted analytically.
FIG. 07
Completed Bridge · Three-Quarter View
Three-quarter view showing the depth of the truss and the deck surface. The bridge was tested to failure under incremental loading, with experimental results compared against the method-of-joints predictions.
