Results Gallery
FIG. 01
Velocity Streamlines
Recirculation zone clearly visible downstream of the step. Reattachment at Xr = 6.53h. Peak velocity 7.768 m/s in the freestream. Near-zero reverse flow in the separation bubble captured by SST k-ω wall treatment.
FIG. 02
Pressure Coefficient Contour (Cp)
Minimum Cp = −0.0131 at the step corner, indicating peak suction. Maximum Cp = 0.325 at the downstream outlet. Pressure recovery gradient consistent with experimental BFS data.
FIG. 03
Grid Independence Study
Reattachment length Xr plotted against element count across five successive meshes. Solution converges at ~53,284 elements. Final analysis used 101,508-element mesh for additional secondary quantity resolution.
FIG. 04
9th-Order Polynomial Curve Fit · RANS vs DNS at x/h = 6
Polynomial fits applied to both RANS and DNS velocity profiles, achieving R² = 1.000 for both. Fits quantify the RANS boundary layer plateau at y/h ≈ 1.0 versus y/h ≈ 2.0 in DNS — a direct measure of the reattachment length overprediction by the RANS model.
FIG. 05
Velocity Profiles U/U₀
RANS vs DNS velocity profiles at x/h = 6 and x/h = 15. RANS boundary layer plateaus at y/h ≈ 1.0 against DNS at y/h ≈ 2.0, confirming the reattachment length overprediction inherent to the SST k-ω closure.
FIG. 06
Turbulent Kinetic Energy k/(U₀)²
RANS peak TKE at x/h = 6 is 0.040 versus DNS 0.012 — a 3.4× overprediction at the shear layer. Consistent with known SST k-ω behaviour in adverse pressure gradient separation flows.
FIG. 07
Structured Quad Mesh · Four-Panel Progression
Mesh refinement sequence showing structured quad elements with wall inflation layers at the step and lower wall. Coarse mesh (top-left) through to the grid-independent 101,508-element analysis mesh. Inflation layer spacing set to capture y⁺ < 1 throughout.
