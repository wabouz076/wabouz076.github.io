ANSYS Fluent · SST k-ω RANS · H7137

Turbulent Flow Over a Backward-Facing Step

↗ View Gallery
ANSYS FluentSST k-ωRANSGrid IndependenceTKE AnalysisCurve Fitting

Steady-state CFD simulation of turbulent separated and reattaching flow over a backward-facing step at Re = 5100, expansion ratio 1.2. The SST k-ω turbulence model was selected for its near-wall accuracy in adverse pressure gradient flows and validated against the DNS benchmark of Le et al. (1997).

A structured quad mesh with wall inflation layers was refined across five successive meshes, tracking reattachment length as the independence monitor. The 101,508-element mesh was selected for all post-processing, providing resolution for secondary quantities including pressure coefficient and turbulent kinetic energy.

A 9th-order polynomial was fitted to both the RANS and DNS velocity profiles at x/h = 6, achieving R² = 1.000 for both. The fits revealed the RANS boundary layer plateau at y/h ≈ 1.0 versus y/h ≈ 2.0 in DNS · a direct quantitative measure of the RANS reattachment length overprediction.

ANSYS Mechanical · MATLAB · H7137

FEA · Planar Truss and Belleville Spring Washer

↗ View Gallery
ANSYS MechanicalMATLABLU DecompositionFEACentral Finite DifferenceNonlinear

Two-part FEA study. The planar truss problem assembles an 8×8 equilibrium system from direction cosines and solves via LU decomposition in MATLAB, then verifies using both LINK180 bar elements and BEAM188 beam elements in ANSYS Mechanical. All eight nodal displacements were reproduced with a maximum error of 0.006%.

The Belleville spring analysis evaluates the spring constant at δ = 3.72 mm using the Norton (1996) force-deflection equation differentiated numerically by a second-order central finite difference scheme. This was compared against 2D axisymmetric and 3D solid FEA models in ANSYS, with large deflection (geometric nonlinearity) enabled throughout.

The 3D model overestimated stiffness by 58.7% relative to the analytical result. The cause was traced to over-constrained lateral boundary conditions at the outer edge, which suppressed natural radial expansion and introduced artificial circumferential stiffness. The 2D axisymmetric model avoids this entirely and provides the more physically consistent result.

MATLAB R2021b · ODE45 · H7137

MATLAB Numerical Modelling Portfolio · Warren Truss and Dynamic Systems

↗ View Source Code
MATLABDirect StiffnessODE45Secant MethodCastiglianoParametric Sweep

Five-problem portfolio built around a 7-node, 11-member Warren truss. Problem 1 assembles a 14×14 global stiffness matrix and solves for all nodal displacements via LU decomposition. Problem 2 sweeps P1 and P3 loads across 10,000 combinations, computing factor of safety for all 11 members at each point and outputting live contour maps with interactive sliders.

Problem 3 loads experimental strain gauge data from a .mat data file, fits a polynomial calibration curve by regression, then uses the secant method to locate the failure load (FoS = 1). Problem 4 computes strain energy member-by-member using the trapezoidal rule and derives nodal displacements via Castigliano's theorem, cross-checked against Problem 1.

Problem 5 integrates the equation of motion of a damped SDOF system using ODE45 with parameters (m = 1200 kg, k = 4×10⁶ N/m, ζ = 0.05), with a live interactive dashboard including resonance detection and dynamic magnification factor output.

Impact of Nozzle Design on Cryogenic Spray Performance

Supervisor: Professor Cyril Crua · In collaboration with two PhD researchers

Subcooled liquid hydrogen (sLH2) has the potential to decarbonise long-haul transportation one of the hardest sectors to electrify. As hydrogen ICE and fuel cell vehicles reach commercial viability, the limiting factor becomes the refuelling infrastructure itself. Boil-off gas (BOG) produced during cryogenic H2 refuelling currently restricts large-scale operations, and nozzle geometry is a key variable governing how much BOG is generated.

This project investigates how nozzle design affects cryogenic spray performance and BOG production. Working alongside two PhD students, the work spans the full engineering cycle: from CAD design and concept selection through metal 3D printing and assembly, to systematic experimental testing on a scaled-down liquid nitrogen rig. Statistical methods will be used to assess repeatability and identify the geometric parameters most strongly linked to reduced BOG.

Project Phases

Design multiple injector nozzle concepts in SolidWorks using pairwise analysis and decision matrices
Manufacture prototypes via metal additive manufacturing (3D printing) and workshop fabrication
Develop and execute test protocols on a scaled liquid nitrogen rig
Characterise BOG production across nozzle geometries using experimental data
Statistical analysis of repeatability and design parameter sensitivity
Iterate on design based on experimental findings
