Part 1 · Full Assembly Renders
FIG. 01
Full Assembly · Front-Left Three-Quarter View
Complete bicycle assembly showing the full drivetrain, wheel spokes, and drop handlebars. The frame geometry was sized to a standard road bike specification and all sub-assemblies — wheels, saddle, stem, and crank — are fully mated.
FIG. 02
Full Assembly · Rear-Right Three-Quarter View
Rear-right perspective showing the chain, rear cassette, disc brake rotor, and saddle assembly. All mates are constrained to reflect realistic component interfaces and spatial clearances.
FIG. 03
Right Side Profile
Clean side elevation highlighting the frame triangle geometry, chain path from chainring to rear sprocket, and the proportions of the fork relative to the main frame.
FIG. 04
Left Side Profile
Left elevation showing the disc brake arrangement on the rear wheel, the saddle post, and the handlebar stem. The frame uses swept tubular profiles throughout to replicate real-world construction.
FIG. 05
Front Elevation
Head-on view showing the drop handlebar width, fork blades, and front tyre profile. The symmetry of the assembly is visible here, with both pedals and the front disc clearly defined.
FIG. 06
Three-Quarter View · Rear Right
Perspective render showing the rear dropout, chain stay, and seat stay junctions. The saddle and seatpost sub-assembly is visible along with the rear wheel hub detail.
FIG. 07
Front-Left View · Suspension Fork Visible
Three-quarter render where the front suspension fork and internal coil spring are visible within the assembly context, showing how the fork sub-assembly integrates with the head tube and steerer.
Part 2 · Suspension Design & Simulation
FIG. 08
Custom Front Suspension Fork · Internal Detail
The custom-designed suspension fork rendered with a transparent outer shell to expose the internal coil spring and damper stack. The spring geometry was determined analytically before being modelled as a parametric helix. Kinematic travel was verified in SolidWorks Motion.
FIG. 09
Frame Simulation · Factor of Safety
SolidWorks Simulation result showing the factor of safety distribution across the bicycle frame under representative loading. The colour map identifies the critical region at node 14668 (FOS min = 0.985) and confirms the rest of the frame is well within safe limits.
